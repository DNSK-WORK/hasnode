[**DNSK.WORK blog**](https://dnsk.work/blog) is the main one. Two formats: tactical posts about real design work – case studies, methods, what actually worked on a specific project – and a series called [#Not\_My\_Type](https://dnsk.work/blog/category/not-my-type/) which is more honest about how the industry actually functions. If you want the long version of how I think about design problems, that's where it is.

[**BLOG@CACM**](https://cacm.acm.org/blogcacm/) is where I publish when a topic needs a more technically rigorous home. The ACM's practitioner blog – the audience is engineers and researchers, so the writing is different. Less industry commentary, more framework and argument.

[**Medium**](https://medium.com/@tanyafonska) is for broader reach on topics that don't belong to a specific platform. Product teams, founders, people adjacent to design work who wouldn't necessarily find the DNSK.WORK blog on their own.

[**HackerNoon**](https://hackernoon.com/u/tanyafonska) is for the more technical takes – tools, systems, AI in design workflows. The audience there overlaps with BLOG@CACM but the format is shorter and the tone is different.

[**LinkedIn**](https://www.linkedin.com/in/tanyafonska/) is where I post about the book. Every week I publish a chapter overview from [*Looks Good to Me: On AI Sycophancy, Context Loss, and Inverted Baselines*](https://play.google.com/store/books/details?id=s8vBEQAAQBAJ) with links to [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/books/details?id=s8vBEQAAQBAJ) and [Apple Books](https://books.apple.com/us/book/looks-good-to-me-on-ai-sycophancy-context-loss-and/id6760121233). That's the most consistent thing I post anywhere.

**This Hashnode blog** is for everything that doesn't fit the above. Notes, observations, short takes, occasional personal things.

A full list of everything I've published outside DNSK.WORK is [here](https://dnsk.work/blog/articles-ive-written-elsewhere-when-editors-said-yes/).
