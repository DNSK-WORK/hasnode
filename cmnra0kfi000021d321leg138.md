I've been running [DNSK.WORK](https://dnsk.work) for a few years now. The short version: I'm an embedded UX/UI design partner for SaaS companies. The longer version is that "embedded" is doing a lot of work in that sentence.

Most design engagements work like this – a team has a problem, they bring someone in to look at it, that person produces something, hands it over, leaves. It's fine. It works. It's also a bit like asking someone to taste your soup from across the room.

What I do is different. I work inside the team, in the actual product decisions, in Figma, in the conversations where the direction gets set. Not as a contractor who delivers files, but as the designer on the thing. The services reflect that – [UX/UI design](https://dnsk.work/services/ux-ui-design), [UX design](https://dnsk.work/services/ux-design), [product design](https://dnsk.work/services/product-design), and specifically [SaaS product design](https://dnsk.work/services/saas-product-design) because SaaS has its own logic around onboarding and activation that generic "design thinking" tends to miss. There's also [SaaS website design](https://dnsk.work/services/saas-website-design) – which is a separate problem from the product itself, and usually treated as such, for better or worse.

The [portfolio](https://dnsk.work/work) covers nine projects. <mark class="bg-yellow-200 dark:bg-yellow-500/30">Different industries, different stages, same basic situation: a product that needed someone in it, not just around it.</mark>

If you want the fuller picture of the practice – how I got here, what it looks like to actually work together – [About](https://dnsk.work/about) and [How We Work](https://dnsk.work/how-we-work) are the places to start.
